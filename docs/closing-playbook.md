# Closing Playbook

## Goal

Turn a useful teardown into a paid sprint without drifting into a vague redesign conversation.

## The Close

Use this close after showing the prospect one or two specific lead leaks:

"The cleanest next step is the Pilot Sprint. I will fix the main conversion path, connect the quote flow, verify form delivery, and confirm tracking inside 72 hours after access is ready. The pilot is $750, and I pause the fee if the page, form, and tracking events are not verified by the deadline."

## Required Before Starting

- Main service to prioritize
- Service area
- Website or CMS access
- Destination email for leads
- Three reviews or proof points
- Logo or simple brand assets
- Analytics access or approval to install tracking
- Deposit or payment confirmation

## Deposit Script

"To reserve the sprint window, I collect the deposit before build work starts. Once that is in, I send the kickoff checklist and begin the Day 1 audit."

## Avoid These Traps

- Do not promise exact lead volume.
- Do not accept unlimited revisions.
- Do not start without access.
- Do not turn the pilot into a full brand strategy project.
- Do not skip form and tracking verification.

## Handoff Upsell

At handoff, offer the care plan:

"The sprint fixes the first conversion path. The care plan keeps the site tested, updated, and improving each month. It covers small edits, form checks, analytics notes, and one small conversion improvement per month."
