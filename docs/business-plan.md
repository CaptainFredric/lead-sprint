# Lead Sprint Business Plan

## Positioning

The business sells small, fixed-scope conversion improvements to local service businesses whose websites are outdated, slow, unclear, or hard to contact from mobile.

The buyer is usually an owner/operator who does not want a large redesign conversation. They want calls, quote requests, and confidence that their website is not leaking leads.

## Core Offer

Name: 72-Hour Lead Sprint

Price: $750 pilot

Promise: A refreshed one-page conversion path with mobile layout, offer clarity, lead form, analytics events, inbox routing, and a 30-day action list.

Delivery window: Three working days after intake and access are complete.

Pilot promise: If the form, mobile page, and analytics events are not verified by the end of day three, the fee pauses until those items are complete.

## Best Initial Niches

Start with businesses where one booked job is worth enough to justify the pilot.

- Plumbers
- HVAC companies
- Roofers
- Electricians
- Dentists
- Med spas
- Chiropractors
- Auto detailers
- Landscapers
- Wedding vendors
- Tutors and test prep providers
- Local legal practices

Avoid early clients with complex booking rules, heavy compliance needs, committees, or unclear authority.

## Pricing Ladder

Pilot Sprint, $750:
One page, one form, analytics events, inbox routing, handoff report.

Growth Sprint, $1,500:
Pilot scope plus review request workflow, email response templates, extra proof section, and two conversion tests.

Care Plan, $250/month:
Monthly edits, uptime check, form test, analytics notes, and small conversion improvements.

Conversion Add-On, $500:
Second service page or landing page for paid ads.

## Why This Can Work

The offer is concrete. The client can understand what changes, when it ships, and what happens if delivery slips.

The sprint is small enough to sell without a long buying cycle, but valuable enough to create a path into retainers.

It uses skills that are already in demand: web cleanup, analytics, AI-assisted copy, integrations, QA, and clear communication.

## 30-Day Execution Plan

Week 1:
Build the landing page, prepare outreach, list 100 prospects, and create a simple audit template.

Week 2:
Contact 20 businesses per day. Send a short personalized note with one observed website issue. Offer three discounted pilots at $500-$750 for proof.

Week 3:
Deliver the first pilots. Capture before/after screenshots, exact fixes, client quotes, and measurable changes.

Week 4:
Raise to standard pricing. Ask each pilot client for a referral and offer the care plan at handoff.

## Daily Sales Routine

1. Find 20 local sites with visible issues.
2. Record one concrete issue per business.
3. Send 10 to 20 personalized emails.
4. Follow up after two business days.
5. Offer a 15-minute teardown call.
6. Close only when access and decision authority are clear.

## Success Metrics

- 100 prospects listed
- 50 personalized emails sent per week
- 5 teardown calls booked per week
- 1 pilot closed per week
- 50 percent of pilots offered a retainer
- 25 percent of pilots converted to care plans

## Red Flags

- Client cannot provide access quickly
- Client wants a custom app instead of a page
- Client wants unlimited revisions
- Client cannot name the main service they want to sell
- Client has no clear contact path or responsible decision-maker
- Client wants guarantees on search rankings or exact revenue

## Upsell Path

At handoff, show the owner:

- What was changed
- What was measured
- Which inquiries or calls can now be tracked
- What should be tested next

Then offer the care plan as continuity, not as an extra chore.
