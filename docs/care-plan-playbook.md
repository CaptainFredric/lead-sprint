# Care Plan Playbook

The care plan turns a finished sprint into predictable monthly revenue. It should feel like a lightweight lead-path maintenance service, not vague agency support.

## Positioning

Sell this at handoff:

> The sprint gets the lead path live and tested. The care plan keeps it healthy with monthly form checks, analytics notes, small edits, and one practical conversion improvement at a time.

Keep the first offer simple:

- $250/month for most pilot clients
- Month-to-month
- One small improvement per month
- Monthly form and phone path test
- Short analytics note
- No unlimited redesign, ad management, or complex CRM work

## Monthly Rhythm

Week 1:

- Send a test lead
- Confirm inbox routing
- Check phone and email links
- Fix urgent breakage

Week 2:

- Review analytics basics
- Note forms, calls, traffic, and top pages
- Identify one practical bottleneck

Week 3:

- Ship one small improvement
- Examples: proof placement, FAQ copy, service-area copy, button wording, review update, trust signal, or broken link fix

Week 4:

- Send the monthly note
- List what was checked
- List what changed
- Name the next improvement
- Ask for one review, photo, offer update, or service detail if needed

## Monthly Report

Use the monthly client report builder on the live page before each renewal touch. The report should include:

- Client name
- Report month
- Form leads reviewed
- Call clicks reviewed
- Care status
- Small improvement shipped
- Main observation
- Next improvement
- Retention health score
- Renewal note

The report is not meant to be a giant analytics deck. It is a compact proof-of-work note that makes the care plan feel active and worth continuing.

## Proof and Referral Loop

After a useful monthly note, use the proof and referral kit generator. The goal is to turn quiet client progress into sales material without overclaiming.

Capture:

- Client name
- Similar buyer to ask for
- Proof permission level
- Best channel
- Visible win
- Simple metric
- Client quote or plain-language reaction

Use the generated kit for three things:

- A testimonial request
- A short case-study snippet
- A referral ask

Keep permission clean. If the client only allows private use, keep the note internal. If they allow anonymized proof, use the result in outreach without the business name. If they approve public proof, use it in proposals and follow-ups as a named mini case study.

## Close Script

Use after the QA report:

> Everything important is live and tested. The next risk is that the lead path slowly drifts: forms stop routing, analytics gets ignored, offers go stale, or the page never gets small improvements. For $250/month, I can keep the form tested, watch the basic numbers, make one useful edit each month, and send you a short note so the site keeps earning.

Then ask:

> Do you want me to keep the lead path maintained for the next 30 days and send the first monthly note?

## Scope Guardrails

Included:

- Monthly form test
- Basic uptime and broken-link check
- Analytics note
- One small conversion edit
- Minor copy/content updates
- Short monthly summary

Not included:

- Full redesigns
- Paid ads
- SEO campaigns
- CRM implementation
- Unlimited revisions
- Emergency support outside the care plan terms

## Retention Math

The care plan converter on the page projects:

- Sprint clients per month
- Handoff-to-care conversion rate
- Monthly fee
- Hours per client
- Projection window
- Monthly recurring revenue
- Annual run rate
- Delivery load

Use it to keep the business honest. If the monthly delivery load gets too high, raise the fee, narrow the scope, or reduce the promised edit cadence.
