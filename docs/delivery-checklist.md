# 72-Hour Delivery Checklist

## Day 0: Intake

Collect:

- Website URL
- Main service to sell
- Service area
- Phone number
- Contact email
- Brand assets
- Hosting or site builder access
- Analytics access, if available
- Three competitors
- Three customer reviews or proof points

Confirm:

- Decision-maker
- Deadline
- Exact pilot scope
- What is out of scope
- Payment timing
- Launch approval process

## Day 1: Audit and Copy

Audit:

- Mobile header and first screen
- Call button visibility
- Quote form visibility
- Page speed basics
- Trust signals
- Reviews
- Service area clarity
- Offer clarity
- Form friction
- Analytics coverage

Write:

- Main headline
- Supporting copy
- Service bullets
- Trust section
- Quote form intro
- Confirmation message
- First response email template

## Day 2: Build

Implement:

- Mobile-first page structure
- Clear call and quote buttons
- Lead form
- Form routing to client inbox
- Spam reduction if supported
- Basic analytics script
- Form submit event
- Click-to-call event
- Thank-you or confirmation state
- Metadata and social preview basics

Check:

- Desktop layout
- Mobile layout
- Tap targets
- Form validation
- Submission delivery
- Phone link
- Email link
- Loading performance basics
- Broken links

## Day 3: Launch and Handoff

Verify:

- Live URL
- Form sends correctly
- Client received test lead
- Analytics records form and call events
- Mobile layout fits
- No obvious console errors
- Favicon and title are set
- Backup or rollback path exists

Deliver:

- Before/after notes
- What changed
- What to watch for 30 days
- Next three conversion improvements
- Care plan offer
- Referral request

Use the launch QA and handoff builder on the live page before sending the final note. Save the QA state locally, copy the handoff report, and only mark the sprint complete when the report reflects the tested live URL, lead destination, completed checks, open checks, and next 30-day actions.

Use the care plan converter immediately after the handoff report. Keep the pitch tied to protection of the lead path: monthly form testing, analytics notes, uptime checks, small edits, and one practical conversion improvement. Do not sell vague ongoing support.

## Handoff Report Structure

Include:

- Client name
- Live URL
- Primary service
- Lead destination
- Launch readiness percentage
- Completed checks
- Open checks
- Client note
- Next 30-day actions
- Care plan offer

The report should make the value of the sprint visible: diagnosis, copy, implementation, form routing, mobile QA, tracking verification, and a practical next-step plan. It should also protect scope by making unfinished items explicit instead of letting them become vague ongoing support.

## Scope Boundaries

Included:

- One page or one focused page refresh
- One lead form
- One primary service offer
- Basic analytics events
- One round of revisions
- Handoff report

Not included:

- Full brand redesign
- Full SEO campaign
- Paid ads management
- Complex booking systems
- Custom CRM development
- Unlimited copy revisions
- Ongoing support without a care plan

## Quality Bar

The sprint is complete only when:

- A ready visitor can understand the offer quickly
- The phone and quote paths are visible on mobile
- The lead form has been tested
- The client has received a test submission
- Analytics events have been verified
- The client has a clear next-step list
