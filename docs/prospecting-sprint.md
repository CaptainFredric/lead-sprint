# Prospecting Sprint

## Purpose

Use this workflow to build the first 100 prospects for the 72-Hour Lead Sprint without getting lost in research.

## Daily Output

Each selling day, aim to produce:

- 20 reviewed prospects
- 20 personalized teardown emails
- 5 follow-ups
- 1 to 3 teardown call asks
- 1 updated pipeline export

Adjust the daily target with the outreach planner on the live page.

## Prospect Sources

Start with businesses where one customer is worth enough to justify a $750 pilot:

- Google Maps service searches
- Chamber of commerce directories
- Local trade association member lists
- Instagram profiles that link to weak websites
- Yelp or Angi listings with outdated websites
- Contractor trucks, yard signs, mailers, and local ads
- Referrals from each completed pilot

## Fast Qualification

Spend no more than three minutes on the first pass.

Good prospect:

- Active local service business
- Website exists and has visible friction
- Main service has meaningful job value
- Owner, manager, or office email is findable
- Recent reviews show the business is alive

Skip:

- Franchise-controlled websites
- No visible decision-maker
- No proof that the business is active
- Heavy compliance or committee buying process
- Businesses where one new job is too low-value

## First Email Formula

Use the prospect console:

1. Add business, website, niche, and job value.
2. Check the visible lead leaks.
3. Save the lead.
4. Copy the email.
5. Personalize the first sentence before sending.

## Follow-Up Rhythm

Day 0:
Send the first teardown note.

Day 2:
Follow up with the same specific issue.

Day 5:
Send a sharper angle, ROI estimate, or mini-proposal.

Day 10:
Close the loop and ask whether website leads are a current priority.

## Pipeline Rules

- Advance a saved prospect after every meaningful touch.
- Do not write a custom proposal until a teardown call is booked or the business shows clear interest.
- Export the CSV at the end of each prospecting block.
- Park low-score leads instead of chasing them.

## Weekly Review

Every Friday, count:

- Emails sent
- Replies received
- Calls booked
- Pilots closed
- Common objections
- Niches with the strongest response

Then update next week's prospect list around the niches and objections that showed signal.
