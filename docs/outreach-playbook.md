# Outreach Playbook

## Prospect Filters

Good prospects:

- Local service business
- Website has a visible mobile or contact problem
- One job is worth at least $300
- Owner or manager email is findable
- Recent reviews show the business is active

Skip prospects:

- No working website
- Franchise with corporate-controlled site
- Heavy enterprise brand standards
- No obvious way to contact a decision-maker
- Very new business with no proof or demand yet

## Quick Audit Checklist

Before sending an email, record one specific issue:

- Main call to action is missing above the fold
- Phone number is hard to tap on mobile
- Quote form is buried
- Page takes too long to understand
- Reviews are not visible near the offer
- Service area is unclear
- There is no tracking plan for calls or forms

## Cold Email 1

Subject: Quick idea for {business}

Hi {name},

I was reviewing {website} and noticed {specific issue}.

For a service like {main service}, that can quietly cost qualified inquiries from mobile visitors.

I run a focused 72-hour Lead Sprint: mobile refresh, clearer offer, quote form, analytics events, and a short handoff report.

The pilot is $750. If the form, mobile page, and analytics events are not verified by the end of day three, the fee pauses until those items are complete.

Would it be worth a 15-minute look this week?

Best,
{your name}

## Follow-Up 1

Subject: Re: Quick idea for {business}

Hi {name},

Quick follow-up. I think {specific issue} is worth fixing because visitors who are ready to book should not have to hunt for the next step.

I can send a short teardown if that is easier than a call.

Best,
{your name}

## Follow-Up 2

Subject: Should I close the loop?

Hi {name},

Should I close the loop on this?

If website leads are not a priority right now, no worries. If they are, I can show you the few changes I would make first.

Best,
{your name}

## Call Structure

Open:
"I looked at the site through the lens of one thing: how quickly a ready-to-buy visitor can request help."

Questions:

- Which service do you most want more of?
- What is an average new job worth?
- How many website inquiries do you get in a typical month?
- Where do form submissions go now?
- Do you know which pages generate calls?
- Who needs to approve the work?
- Can access be provided today if we move forward?

Close:
"The cleanest version is the $750 pilot. Three working days, one service page, quote path, analytics events, and a handoff report. If access is ready, I can start {date}."

## Objection Handles

"We already have a website."

"That is exactly why this is a sprint instead of a rebuild. I am looking for the fastest improvements to the site you already paid for."

"We need SEO."

"SEO matters, but this pilot starts with the visitors you already have. If they cannot request help easily, more traffic just leaks faster."

"Can you guarantee leads?"

"I cannot honestly guarantee demand. I can guarantee the conversion path: mobile layout, clearer offer, working form, analytics events, and verified handoff."

"It is too expensive."

"Totally fair. The reason I price it this way is that the work is fixed, fast, and measurable. If one extra booked job is worth close to the pilot, it usually makes sense."

## Where To Find Prospects

- Google Maps searches by city and service
- Local chamber of commerce directories
- Yelp and Angi listings with outdated linked websites
- Instagram profiles that link to weak sites
- Contractor trucks and yard signs
- Referral requests from the first three pilot clients
